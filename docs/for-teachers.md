# For Teachers

Welcome! This page is your starting point for bringing AIRI into a classroom,
homeschool, science club, or outreach program. Everything here is **free and
public domain** &mdash; adapt it however your students learn best.

!!! abstract "AIRI in one minute"
    Students grow microgreens (fast-growing edible seedlings), measure how they
    grow and how their roots respond to gravity, and share their data with a global
    community of young scientists studying how plants might grow in space.

## Why astrobotany works in the classroom

- **Low cost, low risk.** The core stages need only seeds, water, a tray, and a
  smartphone camera.
- **Fast results.** Microgreens germinate in days, so an investigation fits inside
  a unit &mdash; not a semester.
- **Real data, real science.** Students collect and share genuine measurements, not
  canned worksheet answers.
- **Cross-curricular.** Biology, data literacy, engineering, art, and space science
  all show up naturally.

## Choosing where to start

You do **not** need to run the whole program. Pick a stage that matches your grade
band, time, and budget. The **Classroom Track (Stages I&ndash;IV)** is designed for
K&ndash;12 and needs minimal equipment. The **Extension & Research Track (Stages
V&ndash;XI)** suits advanced high-school, undergraduate, or citizen-science learners.

## Tools your students will use

We are brand-agnostic, but these free tools have a proven classroom track record:

- **[Epicollect5](https://five.epicollect.net/)** &mdash; collect and share data from a
  phone; no account needed for students to contribute.
- **[Easy Leaf Area](https://github.com/heaslon/Easy-Leaf-Area)** &mdash; measure leaf
  area from a photo (Windows, Android, iOS, Linux).
- **Fiji / SmartRoot** &mdash; measure root growth from images (advanced stages).

## Good teaching practice with AIRI

- [ ] Have students keep a **science journal** (paper or a shared doc) recording
      methods, amounts, and timestamps &mdash; this is what makes the work reproducible.
- [ ] Change **only one or two variables** per experiment (light, water, seed type).
- [ ] Photograph samples at the **same time and distance** each day for comparable data.
- [ ] Discuss **data ethics and digital footprints** (Stage I) before sharing online.

## Safety notes

- Use **food-grade seeds** sold for sprouting/microgreens and follow the supplier's
  rinsing guidance to limit microbial growth.
- Microgreens grown for a *data* experiment on agar or treated media are **not for
  eating** &mdash; keep edible and experimental grows clearly separated and labelled.
- Follow your institution's policies for any chemicals used in the research-track stages.
