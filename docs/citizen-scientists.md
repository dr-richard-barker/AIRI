# For Citizen Scientists 🌍🌱

You don't need a classroom or a lab to do real science. With a windowsill, some seeds, and
a phone, **you can grow plants, measure them properly, and add your data to a worldwide
astrobotany project.** Everything here is free and public domain (CC0) — your observations
become part of a shared, reusable dataset that helps everyone understand how plants grow,
on Earth and beyond.

!!! abstract "The idea in one line"
    Grow → measure with your phone → **contribute your data** → see how it joins a global
    picture.

## What you'll need

- **Seeds** — any microgreen (radish, pea, mizuna, broccoli… start easy).
- **A tray + water** — a windowsill spot with light.
- **A smartphone camera.**
- **An [AstroCalibration sticker](https://www.stickermule.com/drb2025)** — a known size + colour
  in each photo so free software can measure your plants. (You can start with a plain ruler.)

## Contribute your data

These open projects accept data from anyone — no account needed to start:

- 🌱 **[Your Favourite Microgreen survey](https://five.epicollect.net/project/your-favorite-microgreen-is)** — vote and tell us why.
- 📊 **[AIRI MicroGreen Easy Leaf Area](https://five.epicollect.net/project/airi-microgreen-easy-leaf-area)** — log leaf-area measurements over time.
- 🌍 **[Urban Microgreens](https://five.epicollect.net/project/urban-microgreens)** — add your grow to the global map.

!!! tip "Why your data matters"
    One windowsill tray is an anecdote; **thousands, shared openly, are a dataset.** Because
    AIRI data is FAIR (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable) and public domain,
    students and researchers around the world can learn from what you grow. Keep a simple
    "science journal" (a note or spreadsheet with dates, amounts, and photos) so your
    contribution is reproducible.
